Human Values

The Gift of Magi & Basic Grammar

📖 Literature: Gift of Magi (O. Henry)

A profound short story about a young married couple, Jim and Della, who are very poor but want to buy each other Christmas gifts. Della sells her beautiful long hair to buy a platinum chain for Jim's prized pocket watch. Jim sells his watch to buy ornamental combs for Della's hair.

Core Theme: The irony of love. Their gifts are practically useless, but represent the highest form of human value and sacrifice, making them "the wisest" (the Magi).

Grammar: Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Name of person, place, thing.
  • Pronoun: Replaces a noun (he, she, it).
  • Adjective: Modifies a noun (beautiful).
  • Verb: Action or state (run, is).
  • Adverb: Modifies verb/adj (quickly).

Vocabulary: Affixes

Prefix: Added before a root.
un- + happy = unhappy
Suffix: Added after a root.
happi + -ness = happiness
Root Word: Base form block.
form -> reform, conform

II

Nature

The Brook & Cohesive Devices

🌊 Literature: The Brook (Tennyson)

A poem written from the perspective of a brook (small river). It details its journey from its source ("haunts of coot and hern") down to the brimming river. Central message contrasts the transient, short life of humans with the eternal, continuous flow of nature.

Key Quote to Remember:

"For men may come and men may go,
But I go on for ever."

Vocabulary Crash

Homophones Same sound, different meaning/spelling (See, Sea).
Homographs Same spelling, different meaning/sound (Lead/Lead).
Homonyms Same spelling & sound, different meaning (Bat/Bat).

Writing: Cohesive Devices (Linkers)

Words that connect ideas in a paragraph.

Addition: moreover, furthermore Contrast: however, although Result: therefore, consequently

III

Biography

Elon Musk & Note-Making

🚀 Biography: Elon Musk

Focus is on reading in detail and making inferences. The text explores his entrepreneurial journey (Zip2, PayPal, SpaceX, Tesla). It highlights traits of perseverance, risk-taking, and visionary thinking.

Grammar: Subject-Verb Agreement

Rule: Singular subjects take singular verbs (-s/-es). Plural subjects take plural verbs.

  • Error: The list of items are on the desk.
  • Correct: The list of items is on the desk. (Subject is "list")

Writing: Note-Making

  • Identifies main ideas and supporting details.
  • Uses abbreviations and symbols (e.g., &, w/, govt).
  • Follows a format (Linear or Non-Linear/Mind Map).
  • No complete sentences. Keep it brief.

Collocations

Words that naturally go together.

Make a mistake (not do a mistake)
Do homework (not make homework)
Heavy rain (not thick rain)

Letters, Resumes & Voices

Format: Official / Formal Letter

[Sender's Address] [Date] [Receiver's Designation] [Receiver's Address] Subject: [Brief 1-line statement of purpose] Respected Sir/Madam, [Para 1: Introduction & State purpose] [Para 2: Details/Explanation] [Para 3: Conclusion/Action Expected] Thanking you, Yours faithfully/sincerely, [Signature] [Name]

Active & Passive Voice

Active: Subject performs action.

The mechanic fixed the car.

Passive: Action is done to subject.

The car was fixed by the mechanic.

* Always maintain tense when converting.

Direct & Indirect Speech

Direct: She said, "I am studying."

Indirect: She said that she was studying.

• Remove quotes.

• Change tense back (Present -> Past).

• Change pronouns (I -> she).

• Time words (now -> then, today -> that day).

Essays & Error Correction

Essay Writing Structure

  1. Introduction: Hook reader, present thesis statement (main argument).
  2. Body Paragraphs (2-3): Topic sentence, evidence/details, concluding sentence. Use linkers.
  3. Conclusion: Restate thesis in new words, summarize points, final thought. No new info.

Text Editing Checklists

  • Articles (a/an/the): Ensure 'the' for specific, a/an for general. (an hour - vowel sound).
  • Prepositions: Depend on in/on/at rules (at time, on day, in month).
  • Tense consistency: Don't randomly switch from past to present.
  • Redundancy: Return back (wrong) -> Return (correct).

Q1. Change to Passive: "The committee approved the new plan."

Ans: The new plan was approved by the committee.

Q2. Edit Error: "He is senior than me in the office."

Ans: He is senior to me in the office.

Q3. Change to Indirect: The teacher said, "Water boils at 100 degrees."

Ans: The teacher said that water boils at 100 degrees. (Universal truth - tense remains unchanged).
