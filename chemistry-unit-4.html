Polymerization

Free Radical Mechanism (Addition)

The backbone of addition polymers (like PVC, Polyethylene). Occurs in three distinct phases powered by highly reactive radicals.

1. Initiation

Create the Spark

R-O-O-R → 2R-O•

Initiator breaks (homolytic cleavage) to form a free radical with an unpaired electron.

2. Propagation

Grow the Chain

R• + CH₂=CH₂ → R-CH₂-CH₂•

Radical attacks a monomer double bond, adding to it and moving the radical to the chain end.

3. Termination

Kill the Radical

R~• + •~R → R~~R

Two growing radicals collide and bond, consuming the unpaired electrons (Coupling or Disproportionation).

Advanced Plastics

Conducting Polymers

Most polymers are insulators. But polymers with Conjugated Double Bonds (alternating single and double bonds) can conduct electricity when doped!

Polyacetylene (The Benchmark)

-CH=CH-CH=CH-CH=CH-

The alternating π (pi) bonds overlap, creating a delocalized electron cloud along the chain.

Doping Process

p-doping (Oxidation) Remove e⁻ (Creates hole)
n-doping (Reduction) Add e⁻
Exam Strategy

Board Exam PYQs

Important Theory

Addition vs Condensation (10M)

Asked 5 Times

Distinguish between Addition (Chain growth) and Condensation (Step growth) polymerization with suitable examples.

Hack: Tabular column! Addition = fast, double bonds break, no byproducts (e.g. Polyethylene). Condensation = slow, functional groups react, byproducts like H2O/HCl released (e.g. Nylon 6,6).

Conducting Polymers (5M)

Asked 4 Times

What are conducting polymers? Give the mechanism of conduction in Polyacetylene / Polyaniline.

Hack: Mention "Conjugation of pi bonds" and "Doping (p-doping or n-doping)". Draw the alternating single/double bond carbon chain of polyacetylene to secure marks.
