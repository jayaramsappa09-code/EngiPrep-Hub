Rubbers & Elastomers

Elastomers preparation & Vulcanization

Co-polymerization maps out monomers like 1,3-Butadiene and Styrene. Natural rubber holds poor mechanical characteristics, which are corrected using vulcanization (heating with sulfur to cross-link chains).

Copolymer

Buna-S prep

Emulsion copolymerization of 1,3-Butadiene ($CH_2=CH-CH=CH_2$) and Styrene ($C_6H_5-CH=CH_2$) in a 3:1 ratio with free radical catalyst and sodium metal.

3 n CH₂=CH-CH=CH₂ + n C₆H₅-CH=CH₂ → -[(-CH₂-CH=CH-CH₂-)₃-CH(C₆H₅)-CH₂-]-n
Homopolymer

Neoprene prep

Synthesized by addition free radical polymerization of Chloroprene (2-chloro-1,3-butadiene) under potassium persulfate catalyst.

n CH₂=C(Cl)-CH=CH₂ → -[-CH₂-C(Cl)=CH-CH₂-]-n (Chloroprene to Neoprene)
Inorganic Rubber

Thiokol rubber

Prepared by condensation of 1,2-dichloroethane ($Cl-CH_2-CH_2-Cl$) and sodium tetrasulfide ($Na_2S_4$), forming disulfide cross bonds.

n Cl-CH₂-CH₂-Cl + n Na₂S₄ → -[-CH₂-CH₂-S-S(=S)(=S)-]-n + 2n NaCl

Ecological Materials

Biodegradable Polymers (PLA & PGA)

Modern synthetic plastics remain intact for centuries. Biodegradable macromolecules like Polylactic Acid (PLA) and Polyglycolic Acid (PGA) possess ester linkages that break down easily under microbial or soil compost water actions.

Corn Derived

Polylactic Acid (PLA) synthesis

Lactic acid ($CH_3-CH(OH)-COOH$) undergoes dehydration to form highly stable cyclic dimer Lactide. Ring-Opening Polymerization (ROP) catalyzed by **Tin(II) Octoate** yields PLA chains.

Lactic Acid → Lactide (Cyclic) → -[-O-CH(CH₃)-CO-]-n
Medical Sutures

Polyglycolic Acid (PGA) synthesis

Glycolic acid ($CH_2(OH)-COOH$) is dehydrated to Glycolide (cyclic dimer), followed by ring-opening under coordination catalysts to yield medical-grade absorbable PGA sutures.

Glycolic Acid → Glycolide (Cyclic) → -[-O-CH₂-CO-]-n
