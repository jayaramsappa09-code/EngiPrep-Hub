Orthographic Projections (Points, Lines, Planes) Survival Kit

Orthographic quadrants, first angle projection rules, projection of points, straight lines inclined to both HP & VP, locus trace lines, and projections of regular planes.

Multiview Projective Geometry

Understand the core principles of first-angle projection. Master how points and inclined lines translate on horizontal (HP) and vertical (VP) reference quadrants.

Key Definitions

Orthographic Projection

A method of drawing where points of an object are projected perpendicular to projection planes.

Locus of End Point

The locus representing the path of the endpoint of an inclined line as it rotates through reference planes.

Core Formulas & Logic

True Length vs Apparent

Apparent Front length = True Length * cos θ

XY Reference line splits

Front views (a') sit above XY ; Top views (a) sit below XY

Board Exam PYQs

Inclined Line Projection (14 Marks)

Line AB, 75mm long, has end A 15mm above HP, 20mm in front of VP. The line is inclined at 30° to HP and 45° to VP. Draw projections and find apparent inclinations.

Inclined Regular Plane (10 Marks)

Draw projections of a regular hexagonal plane of 30mm side, having its surface inclined at 45° to HP and one edge resting on HP inclined 30° to VP.

1-Day Prep Strategies

Revision Points

  • In First-Angle, Front View (fv) is above XY, Top View (tv) is below
  • True length (TL) rotation gives the apparent front/top views on their local loci
  • Locus lines of endpoints are always horizontal reference bands

Memory Trick

"a' is Front View. a is Top View. fv ABOVE, tv BELOW."

Exam-Time Strategy

Confirm notations: front view ends with prime (a', b'), top view has no prime (a, b). Draw horizontal locus lines light and parallel.

Interactive Viva Cards

First-Angle Projection?

In JNTUK, object is placed in quadrant 1: above HP and in-front of VP. Apparent top views land below the horizontal reference line XY, front views land above.

Locus Method for Lines?

Inclined lines appear foreshortened. By rotating the true length of a line to reference planes, we project apparent lengths onto their respective locus grids.
