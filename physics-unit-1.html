Unit 1: Wave Optics.

Interference of light, conditions for interference, and the science behind Newton's Rings and Michelson's Interferometer.

01. Principle of Superposition

When two or more light waves of the same frequency and constant phase difference overlap at a point, the resultant intensity is not just the sum of individual intensities. This redistribution of light energy is called Interference.

Resultant Amplitude R = √(a₁² + a₂² + 2a₁a₂ cos φ)

02. Newton's Rings

A very important 7-mark question in JNTUK examinations. It involves the interference of light between a plano-convex lens and a glass plate.

  • • Dark Ring Diameter: Dₙ² = 4nλR
  • • Bright Ring Diameter: Dₙ² = 2(2n-1)λR
  • • Wavelength determination: λ = (Dₙ₊ₘ² - Dₙ²) / 4mR

03. Exam Important Points

Must Draw

  • • Michelson Interferometer Setup Diagram

Calculation Tips

  • • Convert all units to Meters (1 Å = 10⁻¹⁰ m)
  • • Pay attention to 'Refractive Index' (μ) values
  • • Remember n=1, 2, 3... order of rings
