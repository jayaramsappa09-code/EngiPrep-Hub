Introduction to Wave Optics

Physical optics or Wave Optics is a branch of physics studying light properties where ray approximation of geometrical optics is invalid ($\lambda \approx 10^{-7}\text{ m}$).

The unit revolves around three phenomena: Interference, Diffraction, and Polarization.

Key Concepts of Interference & Superposition

When two light waves overlap, the instantaneous algebraic sum defines the physical state.

φ = (π / λ) * Δ
I = I₁ + I₂ + 2 √(I₁ I₂) cos(φ)

Important Definitions

01. Coherent Sources: Sources with identical wavelength and frequency that maintain a constant phase difference.

02. Division of Amplitude: Dividing the amplitude of a single ray into two parts via reflection/refraction.

03. Stokes' Treatment: Reflection from denser medium causes an abrupt phase change of π.
