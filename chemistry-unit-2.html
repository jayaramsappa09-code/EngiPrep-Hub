Zero Resistance

Superconductors & Meissner Effect

Materials that lose absolutely all electrical resistance below a certain critical temperature (TC). Because electrons encounter zero friction, a current can flow infinitely without a power source.

Critical Temperature (TC)

The exact temperature at which the material transitions from a normal conductor to a superconductor. Above TC: normal. Below TC: zero resistance.

The Meissner Effect

The complete expulsion of magnetic flux from the interior of a superconductor as it transitions below TC. This causes Magnetic Levitation.

Nanomaterials (C₆₀, CNTs, Graphene)

Graphene

A single 2D layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice. It is the basic structural element for all other graphitic materials.

  • 200x stronger than steel
  • Exceptional conductor
  • Zero bandgap semiconductor
Rolled Cylinder

Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs)

Graphene sheets rolled into seamless cylinders. Types: SWCNT (Single-Wall) and MWCNT (Multi-Wall).

  • Arc-discharge synthesis
  • Laser ablation method
  • CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition)

Fullerenes (C₆₀)

Buckyballs! Hollow cages of 60 carbon atoms shaped like a soccer ball (12 pentagons, 20 hexagons).

  • Drug delivery carriers
  • Superconductors (when doped)
  • Lubricants

Important Set

Carbon Nanotubes Synthesis (10M)

Asked 4 Times

Explain the preparation of Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) using the CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) method with a neat diagram.

Hack: The CVD diagram is very simple: draw a horizontal tube furnace, a quartz boat inside containing catalyst (Ni/Co/Fe), and gas inlets (Hydrocarbon + Argon flow). Bullet your steps.

Supercapacitors vs Batteries (5M)

Asked 3 Times

Define Supercapacitors. Write the mechanism and differences between a supercapacitor and a conventional battery.

Hack: Always draw a comparison table. Batteries = High Energy Density (run long time), slow charge. Supercapacitors = High Power Density (fast burst), fast charge.

Differentiate SWCNTs and MWCNTs
