Syllabus Scope Definition

UNIT III INTRODUCTION: Electrochemistry

This unit establishes the boundary between chemical structures and engineering thermodynamics. You will transition from standard reduction potentials (Nernst equation) to operational instrumentation methods such as **Potentiometry (Redox titrations)** and **Conductometry (Acid-Base titrations)**.

Furthermore, we investigate the energy conversion models behind portable grids: the highly stable Calomel electrode, multi-cell **Zinc-Air batteries**, **Lithium-Ion systems**, standard **$H_2-O_2$ Fuel cells**, and **Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC)** designed for modern zero-emission vehicles.

Syllabus Topics Core Concept Library

Concept 1

Electrochemical Cell

A device capable of either generating electrical energy from chemical reactions (Galvanic/Voltaic) or facilitating chemical changes via electricity (Electrolytic cell).

Structure formulation: Anode (site of Oxidation, negative charge) and Cathode (site of Reduction, positive charge), bridged by an ion-conducting electrolyte salt bridge.

$$\text{Cell Reaction Representation: } \text{Zn(s)} \mid \text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) \parallel \text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) \mid \text{Cu(s)}$$

Concept 2

Nernst Equation formulation

An equation that relates the reduction potential of an electrochemical cell (or half-cell) to the temperature and activities/concentrations of chemical species involved.

Key equation: Calculates non-standard cell electromotive potential with high mathematical accuracy.

$$E_{\text{cell}} = E^{\circ}_{\text{cell}} - \frac{2.303 RT}{nF} \log \left(\frac{[\text{Anode Ions}]}{[\text{Cathode Ions}]}\right)$$

At standard $25^{\circ}\text{C}$ ($298\text{ K}$), the constant factor reduces to $0.0592 \text{ V}$.

Concept 3

Potentiometry & Potentiometric Titrations

An electrochemical analysis technique where the potential difference between a reference electrode and an indicator electrode is measured as a function of titrant volume added.

Redox Titration model: Involving $Fe^{2+}$ vs $K_2Cr_2O_7$. The equivalence point manifests as an abrupt vertical inflection on the Potential vs. Volume curve ($dE/dV$ first derivative spike).

Concept 4

Concept of Conductivity & Conductivity Cell

Conductivity ($\kappa$) measures an electrolyte solution's ability to conduct electrical current. It represents the reciprocal of resistivity ($\rho$).

Cell Constant ($G^{\ast}$): Ratio of distance between electrodes ($l$) to area ($a$), measured utilizing a Wheatstone bridge framework inside a specialized glass conductivity cell.

$$\kappa = G \cdot G^{\ast} \implies \kappa = \left(\frac{1}{R}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{l}{a}\right)$$

Concept 5

Conductometric Titrations (Acid-Base)

Measurements of electrolytic conductivity variations as the titration progress alters the ionic species concentrations.

Acid-Base Example: Strong Acid (HCl) vs Strong Base (NaOH). The conductance decreases sharply first as hyper-mobile $H^+$ ions are replaced by slower $Na^+$, reach a minimum at equivalence, and then rise due to free highly mobile $OH^-$ ions.

Concept 6

Potentiometric Sensors with Examples

Electrochemical sensors that convert specific chemical activity changes into measurable indicator electrode potentials under zero-current conditions.

Primary Example: The standard **Glass pH electrode** acts as a potentiometric sensor selectively permeable to hydronium ions ($H^+$), generating potential differences directly proportional to pH values.

Concept 7

Reference Electrodes: NHE & Calomel

Electrodes whose potential remains completely stable and independent of the sample chemistry, acting as a potential baseline reference.

NHE (Normal Hydrogen Electrode): Gas electrode, bubbling $H_2$ gas at $1\text{ atm}$ over solid platinum foil immersed in $1\text{ M } H^+$ solution. Potential is arbitrarily defined as $0.000\text{ V}$.

Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE): Secondary reference based on liquid mercury, solid mercurous chloride paste ($Hg_2Cl_2$ paste), and saturated aqueous $KCl$ electrolyte. Highly practical with potential of $+0.242\text{ V}$.

Concept 8

Primary Cells: Zinc-Air Battery

Primary (non-rechargeable) battery utilizing zinc oxidation at the anode alongside oxygen reduction drawn directly from the atmosphere at a gas-diffusion cathode.

Anode half reaction: $$\text{Zn} + 2\text{OH}^- \rightarrow \text{ZnO} + \text{H}_2\text{O} + 2e^-$$

Cathode half reaction: $$\text{O}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} + 4e^- \rightarrow 4\text{OH}^-$$

Key Benefit: Infinite cathode reactant storage because oxygen is taken from free surrounding air!
Concept 9

Secondary Cells: Lithium-Ion Batteries

High energy-density rechargeable storage device operating via intercalation/de-intercalation of Lithium ions ($Li^+$) between a layered graphite anode ($LiC_6$) and a lithium metal oxide cathode ($LiCoO_2$).

$$\textbf{Anode: } \text{LiC}_6 \rightleftharpoons 6\text{C} + \text{Li}^+ + e^-$$

$$\textbf{Cathode: } \text{CoO}_2 + \text{Li}^+ + e^- \rightleftharpoons \text{LiCoO}_2$$

The process of discharging transfers ions from graphite to oxide; charging reverses the direction.

Concept 10

Fuel Cells: H2-O2 & PEMFC

Continuous conversion cells that produce electricity from the constant feed of oxidation gas fuel (hydrogen) and reduction gas oxidizer (oxygen).

PEMFC (Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cell): Employs an ultra-thin hydrogen-ion selective polymeric membrane (commonly Nafion) as the solid acidic electrolyte matrix operating at low temperatures.

$$\textbf{PEMFC Reactions: } \text{H}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{H}^+ + 2e^- \quad \text{(Anode, Platinum Catalyst)}$$

$$\frac{1}{2}\text{O}_2 + 2\text{H}^+ + 2e^- \rightarrow \text{H}_2\text{O} \quad \text{(Cathode)}$$

Cheat Sheet

High-Yield Formula Sheet & Memory Hacks

1. Nernst potential equation

$$E = E^{\circ} - \frac{0.0592}{n} \log_{10} \frac{[\text{Red}]}{[\text{Ox}]}$$

• Valid solely at $298\text{ K}$ ($25^{\circ}\text{C}$).
• $n$ represents moles of electrons transferred.
• $E^{\circ}$ is standard cell potential.

2. Cell Potential (EMF)

$$E_{\text{cell}} = E_{\text{RHS}} - E_{\text{LHS}}$$ $$E_{\text{cell}} = E_{\text{cathode}} - E_{\text{anode}}$$

• Both potentials must represent reduction potentials.
• If Oxidation potential is given, reverse the sign!

3. Conductivity cell parameter

$$G^{\ast} = \frac{l}{a}$$ $$\kappa = \frac{G^{\ast}}{R} = G^{\ast} \cdot G$$

• $G$: conductance in Siemens ($S$ or $\Omega^{-1}$)
• $G^{\ast}$: cell constant measuring in $\text{cm}^{-1}$
• $\kappa$: Specific conductivity in $\text{S cm}^{-1}$.

🧠

Mnemonic Hack: AnOx RedCat Plus NHE

Remember: AnOx = **An**ode is **Ox**idation. RedCat = **Red**uction occurs at the **Cat**hode. Saturated Calomel is ALWAYS $+0.242\text{ V}$ relative to NHE at $0.00\text{ V}$.

Highly Probability Derivation

Thermodynamic Derivation of Nernst Equation

Step-by-step mathematical bridge from free energy parameters to standard concentrations.

Step 1: Write down Gibbst thermodynamic relation
Free energy changes in non-equilibrium conditions relate to reaction quotients ($Q$) via:
$$\Delta G = \Delta G^{\circ} + RT \ln Q \quad \text{--- (Equation 1)}$$

Step 2: Connect thermodynamic energy to electrical EMF output
We know that the maximum electronic work done represents mechanical charges, so:
$$\Delta G = -nFE_{\text{cell}} \quad \text{and} \quad \Delta G^{\circ} = -nFE^{\circ}_{\text{cell}}$$

Step 3: Substitute terms back into Equation 1
$$-nFE_{\text{cell}} = -nFE^{\circ}_{\text{cell}} + RT \ln Q$$

Step 4: Isolate Cell Potential via division
Divide both sides through by $-nF$:
$$E_{\text{cell}} = E^{\circ}_{\text{cell}} - \frac{RT}{nF} \ln Q$$

Step 5: Convert natural logarithm to log base 10 at 298 Kelvin
Substitute $R = 8.314 \text{ J mol}^{-1}\text{K}^{-1}$, $F = 96485 \text{ C mol}^{-1}$, and $T = 298.15\text{ K}$:
$$\frac{2.303 \times 8.314 \times 298.15}{96485} = 0.05916$$
$$E_{\text{cell}} = E^{\circ}_{\text{cell}} - \frac{0.0592}{n} \log_{10} Q$$

Exam Solver Layouts

Board Solved Numerical Lab

JNTUK Model 1

Calculate the potential of a Daniell cell operating at $298\text{ K}$ with $[\text{Zn}^{2+}] = 0.1\text{ M}$ and $[\text{Cu}^{2+}] = 0.01\text{ M}$. Standard potential $E^{\circ} = 1.10\text{ V}$.

Explicit Steps:
1. **Identify the Net Reaction Flow:**
$$\text{Zn(s)} + \text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) \rightarrow \text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Cu(s)}$$
Here,Zinc loses 2 electrons ($n = 2$), Copper gains 2 electrons.

2. **Write Nernst Equation quotient:**
$$Q = \frac{[\text{Zn}^{2+}]}{[\text{Cu}^{2+}]} = \frac{0.1}{0.01} = 10$$

3. **Compute values:**
$$E_{\text{cell}} = E^{\circ}_{\text{cell}} - \frac{0.0592}{n} \log_{10} Q$$
$$E_{\text{cell}} = 1.10 - \frac{0.0592}{2} \log_{10}(10)$$
$$E_{\text{cell}} = 1.10 - 0.0296(1) = 1.0704\text{ V}$$

Final Boxed Answer: E_cell = 1.07 V
JNTUK Model 2

Using a conductivity cell with constant $0.5\text{ cm}^{-1}$, an electrolyte displays a resistance of $50\ \Omega$. Calculate its explicit conductivity ($\kappa$).

Explicit Steps:
1. **Identify the parameters:** Cell constant $G^{\ast} = 0.5\text{ cm}^{-1}$, Resistance $R = 50\ \Omega$ (Conductance $G = 1/50 = 0.02\text{ S}$).
2. **Formulate the specific conductivity:**
$$\kappa = G \cdot G^{\ast} \implies \kappa = \frac{G^{\ast}}{R}$$
$$\kappa = \frac{0.5}{50} = 0.01\text{ S cm}^{-1}$$

Final Boxed Answer: Specific Conductivity = 0.01 S/cm (or 10 mS/cm)

Question 1

Why is Calomel preferred over NHE in laboratory setups?

Click to Reveal ➔

Answer: NHE requires constant setup of explosive, pure hydrogen gas bubbled at exactly $1\text{ atm}$ alongside highly corrosive acid. Calomel SCE is rugged, non-explosive, and solid paste.

Gold Answer 👍
Question 2

What electrolyte is used in Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC)?

Click to Reveal ➔

Answer: An ultra-thin polymeric membrane polymer, typically **Nafion** (sulfonated tetrafluoroethylene copolymer), which selectively passes $H^+$ protons but bars electrons.

Topper Answer 👍
Question 3

Explain why Zn-Air battery is classified as primary?

Click to Reveal ➔

Answer: Its zinc metal anode slowly oxidizes to ZnO irreversibly. Because Zinc has high passivation limits, the cell reactions are difficult to reverse safely, making it a primary power source.
