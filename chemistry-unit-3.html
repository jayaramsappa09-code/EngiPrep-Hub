Formula Mastery

Nernst Equation Calculator

Used to calculate the cell potential at non-standard conditions (when concentration is not 1M or temperature is not 298K).

Standard Form

E = E° - 0.0591 / n × log[P]/[R]
  • E = Cell Potential under given conditions
  • E° = Standard Cell Potential
  • 0.0591 = Constant at 298K (25°C)
  • n = Number of electrons transferred
  • [P]/[R] = [Product Ion] / [Reactant Ion]

Nernst Equation Derivation (10M)

Asked 6 Times

Derive the Nernst equation for a single electrode potential and cell potential. Calculate the EMF of cell exactly.

Hack: Start from thermodynamic relation: ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q. Then substitute ΔG = -nFE. Divide the entire equation by -nF to get the final formula. Easiest 10 marks if you show these 3 steps clearly.

Calomel Electrode (5M)

Asked 3 Times

Explain the construction and working of a Calomel Electrode with a neat diagram.

Hack: It's a Secondary Reference Electrode. Hg(l) at bottom, coated with paste of Hg2Cl2 (Calomel), filled with KCl solution. Write the formula: ½ Hg2Cl2 + e- → Hg + Cl-.

Why use a Salt Bridge?

What is SHE Potential?

The Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE) has a standard potential assigned exactly 0.00 Volts at all temperatures. It's the baseline for all others.
