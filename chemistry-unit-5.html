The Governing Equation

A = εbc
  • A = Absorbance A: 0.50
  • ε (epsilon) = Molar Absorptivity (Constant for a specific substance)
  • b / l = Path Length of Cuvette
  • c = Concentration of the Solution
Exam Note: Absorbance (A) is directly proportional to both concentration and path length. Transmittance (T) decreases exponentially as A increases!

Studies vibrational transitions of bonds (stretching & bending). The ultimate tool to identify Functional Groups (like -OH, C=O).

Beer-Lambert Law (10M)

Must Learn

State and derive the Beer-Lambert law. What are its limitations?

Hack: A = εbc. Limitations to memorize: Only works for dilute solutions (< 0.01M), scattering of light disrupts readings, and deviations occur if solute associates/dissociates.

Chromatography Basics (5M)

Explain the principle of Chromatography. Differentiate between Mobile phase and Stationary phase.

Hack: Principle is "Differential adsorption or partition of components between two phases". Stationary = solid/liquid fixed on column. Mobile = Gas/Liquid moving through it.
