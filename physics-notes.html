Topper's Corner: High-Yield Q&A

Short Answers (2 Marks)

Q1. State the principle of Superposition.

Ans: When two or more waves overlap, the resultant displacement at any point is the vector sum of individual displacements.

Q2. What are Newton's Rings?

Ans: Circular interference fringes formed between a plano-convex lens and a glass plate due to varying air film thickness.

Q3. Define Coordination Number.

Ans: The number of nearest neighboring atoms surrounding a particular atom. e.g., BCC = 8, FCC = 12.

Q4. What is Meissner Effect?

Ans: The expulsion of magnetic flux from a superconductor when it is cooled below its critical temperature.

Descriptive Analysis (10 Marks)

The Hall Effect

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in a transverse magnetic field, an electric field is generated perpendicular to both current and magnetic field.

• Determine type of semiconductor
• Measure carrier concentration
• Measure magnetic flux density
• Determine carrier mobility

Fermi-Dirac Distribution

Determines the probability of an electron occupying an energy state E at temperature T based on quantum statistics.

f(E) = 1 / [exp((E - Ef) / kT) + 1]
