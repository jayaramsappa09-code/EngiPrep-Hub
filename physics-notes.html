JNTUK Nov 2024 Exam

Radius of Curvature in Newton's Rings

10 Marks (Unit 1)

Prove that the diameter D_n of the nth dark ring formed in the interference is proportional to the square root of natural numbers. Detail the Experimental curvature radius setup.

Mathematical Steps:

1. Path difference is Δ = 2μt cos(r) + λ/2. For normal incidence and air film: Δ = 2t + λ/2 (due to electromagnetic wave reflection phase shift of π).

2. For dark ring condition, path difference is: 2t + λ/2 = (2n+1)λ/2 → 2t = nλ.

3. From geometry of plano-convex lens of radius R: t = r²/2R = D_n²/8R.

4. Sub t into step 2: 2(D_n²/8R) = nλ → D_n² = 4nλR → D_n = 2 * √(nλR).

👉 Scoring Trap: Do not miss explaining the phase change of λ/2 by reflection from the denser boundary plate.

JNTUK April 2024 Exam

Setting Schrödinger Time Independent Equation

10 Marks (Unit 2)

Set up the stationary wave equation for a free particle inside an infinite potential well depth barriers and explain physical normalization constants.

Mathematical Steps:

1. Starting from 1D Schrodinger Eq: d²Ψ/dx² + (2m/ℏ²)(E - V)Ψ = 0.

2. Inside the box V = 0, so: d²Ψ/dx² + k²Ψ = 0 where k² = 2mE/ℏ².

3. General Solution is: Ψ(x) = A sin(kx) + B cos(kx).

4. Boundary Condition: Ψ(0) = 0 → B = 0; Ψ(L) = 0 → sin(kL) = 0 → k = nπ/L.

5. Normalizing ∫|Ψ|² dx = 1 gives A = √(2/L). Thus, wave function is: Ψ(x) = √(2/L) sin(nπx/L).

JNTUK Nov 2023 Exam

Hall Effect Current Transport & Direct Applications

10 Marks (Unit 3)

Explain Hall Effect. Derive the formula of Hall Coefficient constant and illustrate its applications in electrical instrumentation.

Mathematical Steps:

1. Force balance at equilibrium: Lorentz magnetic force equals electrostatic force: e·E_H = e·v_d·B → E_H = v_d·B.

2. Current density equation is: J = n·e·v_d → v_d = J / (n·e).

3. Substitute v_d into force balance: E_H = J·B / (n·e) = R_H·J·B where R_H = 1/(n·e).

4. Measurements: R_H = (V_H * w) / (I * B) where w is width of sample.

Lab Practical & Concept Viva Review

Prepare instantly for JNTUK external lab examiners

Q1. Why are the interference fringes in Newton's rings circular?

because the locus of points of same thickness of the wedge-shaped air-film enclosed between the plano-convex lens curvature and glass plate is concentric circles around the core point of contact.

Q2. What is the physical significance of wave function Ψ?

Ψ itself represents amplitude probability without direct physical reality. However, the square modulus |Ψ|² represents the probability distribution density of finding the particle at coordinates x, y, z in space.

Q3. What is the cause of population inversion in He-Ne lasers?

Helium atoms are excited to metastable states via atomic electron collision (gas discharge pump), and transfer active energy to Neon ground level atoms during inelastic collision, creating population inversion in Neon.

Q4. Detail the Meissner effect expulsion mechanisms.

When a material is in its superconducting state beneath Tc, it expels all internal magnetic lines of flux from its body, creating perfect diamagnetism with optical magnetic susceptibility χ = -1.
