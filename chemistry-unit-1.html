Core Principle

Quantum Mechanics & Wave Forms

Classical physics fails at the atomic scale. Electrons don't orbit like planets; they exist as probability waves. Quantum Mechanics provides the mathematical foundation to predict where an electron is likely to be found, determining how atoms bond and react.

The Core Equation

ĤΨ = EΨ

Ψ

Wave Function (Psi)

Ψ²

Probability Density

The probability of finding an electron in a specific volume. This gives us orbital shapes (s, p, d, f).

Core Theory

Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT)

When atomic orbitals overlap, they merge to form new molecular orbitals. The number of new orbitals formed equals the number of combining atomic orbitals. They divide into low-energy Bonding MOs and high-energy Antibonding MOs.
