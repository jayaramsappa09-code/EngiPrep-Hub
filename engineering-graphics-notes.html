Representative Fraction (R.F)

RF = (Length of Drawing) / (Actual Length)

  • Always convert drawing and object to the same units.
  • Length of Scale (LOS) = RF × Maximum Length.

Isometric Scaling

Iso Length = True Length × (√2 / √3)

Rule: Isometric length is ~0.816 times the true length. E.g. 100mm -> 81.6mm.

Ellipse Eccentricity

e = Distance to Focus / Distance to Directrix

  • e < 1 : Ellipse (e.g. 2/3)
  • e = 1 : Parabola
  • e > 1 : Hyperbola (e.g. 3/2)

Orthographic Quadrants

1st Angle: Object between Observer & Plane.
Top View goes BELOW xy line. Front View ABOVE xy.

3rd Angle: Plane between Observer & Object. (Standard US CAD)
Top View goes ABOVE xy line.

Frequently repeated drawing problems mapped with topper diagram strategies.

Unit I - 15 Marks (Repeated 5x)

Construct a hyperbola when the distance of focus from directrix is 50mm and eccentricity is 3/2.

👇
Strategy:

1. Draw directrix DD' and axis AB. Mark Focus F at 50mm from DD'.
2. e=3/2 means Vertex V is 3 parts from F and 2 parts from Directrix. (Total 5 parts).
3. VF = 30mm, VA = 20mm.
4. Draw VE perpendicular to axis, VE = VA. Draw line A-E extended.
5. Erect ordinates along axis, project to A-E line, transfer height to arcs.
6. Use French curve for smooth branch.
Unit III - 15 Marks (R20 & R23 Specimen)

Pentagonal prism axis inclined at 45° to HP and parallel to VP. Base edge 30mm, axis 60mm.

👇
Strategy (Rotation Method):

1. Initial State: Assume axis perpendicular to HP. True shape of pentagon in Top View (resting on corner or edge, check question).
2. Draw TV (Pentagon) and project FV (Rectangle).
3. Tilt FV: Redraw FV so its axis is 45° to XY line.
4. Final TV: Project lines down from tilted FV, and horizontally from original TV. Find intersections.
5. Check visibility: Outer boundary complete. Line passing through mass is dotted.

🃏 VIVA_FLIP_CARDS //

Lab external & internal short answers.

What is the difference between Isometric View and Projection?

View uses true dimensions. Projection uses isometric scale (0.816 * true length).

Why are sectional views used?

To reveal hidden internal features of a complex object that are unclear with hidden (dotted) lines alone.

What is an involute?

The path traced by the free end of a taut string as it was unwound from a base circle or polygon.

Define Representative Fraction (RF).

Ratio of length of object on drawing to actual length of the object, irrespective of units.
