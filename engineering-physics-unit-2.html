Lasers & Fiber Optics
Complete Exam Survival Kit

Curated specifically for JNTUK R23 Engineering Physics. Simplified mathematical flow, precise terminology, scoring diagrams, and practical solutions.

Laser Action Principles

Spontaneous vs Stimulated

Spontaneous emission is random; stimulated emission is forced by an incident photon, resulting in a coherent, monochromatic, and phase-matched secondary photon.

Population Inversion

The state where atoms in excited state ($N_2$) outnumber those in ground state ($N_1$). Crucial for net amplification.

Laser Construction: He-Ne & Ruby

1. He-Ne Laser

  • Medium: He (~10) : Ne (~1) mixture.
  • Pumping: Electrical discharge.
  • Mechanism: He atoms transfer energy to Ne.
  • Output: 632.8 nm (Red).

2. Ruby Laser (Three-level)

  • Medium: Al2O3 doped with Cr³⁺ ions.
  • Pumping: Optical (Xenon flash tube).
  • Nature: Pulsed output.
  • Challenges: High power threshold for PN.

Optical Fiber & NA

Fundamentals: Light propagates via Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

NA = sin(θ_a) = √(μ₁² - μ₂²)

Step-Index Fiber

Constant refractive index in core.

Graded-Index Fiber

Refractive index varies (parabolic) to reduce signal dispersion.

Key Formulas

NA = √(μ₁² - μ₂²)
V = (2πa/λ) * NA

Solved Board PYQs

Q: Distinguish between spontaneous and stimulated emission.

Spontaneous is random, low intensity; Stimulated is forced, coherent, high intensity.

Q: Why is population inversion necessary?

To achieve gain: $N_2 > N_1$ ensures more stimulated emission than absorption occurs during photon interaction.

Q: Define Numerical Aperture.

NA represents the light-collecting ability of a fiber; it is the sine of the acceptance angle.
