RMS Value (Sine Wave)

Irms = Imax / √2 = 0.707 Imax

  • Form Factor = RMS / Avg = 1.11
  • Peak Factor = Max / RMS = 1.414

Transformer EMF Equation

E = 4.44 × f × Φm × N

f is freq, Φm is max flux, N is turns.

AC Power Components

P = VI cosφ (Active, W)
Q = VI sinφ (Reactive, VAR)
S = VI (Apparent, VA)

Power Factor = cosφ = P / S = R / Z

Boolean Algebra Laws

A + AB = A (Absorption)
A + A'B = A + B
(A+B)' = A' · B' (DeMorgan's)

Unit I - 10 Marks (Repeated 4x)

Derive the expression for RMS value and Average Value of an alternating quantity.

👇
Strategy:

1. Start with i = Im * sin(θ) where θ = ωt.
2. Average: Integrate over half cycle (0 to π). Avg = (1/π) ∫ Im*sin(θ) dθ = 2*Im/π = 0.637*Im.
3. Root Mean Square: Square the wave, find mean over full cycle (0 to 2π), then root.
4. RMS = √ [ (1/2π) ∫ (Im*sinθ)² dθ ]. Use identity sin²θ = (1-cos2θ)/2.
5. Result: Irms = Im / √2 = 0.707*Im.
(Write clean steps, box final answers).
Unit V - 5 Marks (R20/R23 Specimen)

Explain the operation of Full Wave Bridge Rectifier with waveforms.

👇
Strategy:

1. Diagram is 50% of marks. Draw 4 diodes (D1, D2, D3, D4) in bridge formation.
2. Positive half-cycle: D1 and D2 are forward biased (conducting), D3/D4 are reverse.
3. Negative half-cycle: D3 and D4 are forward biased (conducting), D1/D2 are reverse.
4. Note that current direction through load resistor RL is SAME in both cycles.
5. Draw input AC sine wave, and output pulsating DC wave (m-shapes).
6. Efficiency = 81.2%, Ripple Factor = 0.48.

🃏 VIVA_FLIP_CARDS //

Labs & External Viva Short Answers.

Why does a transformer only work on AC?

Transformer works on Faraday's law of induction which requires changing magnetic flux. DC provides constant flux, so no induced EMF. (It can also burn out).

Define Power Factor.

Cosine of the angle between voltage and current (cosφ). Also defined as ratio of Active power to Apparent power (P/S).

Difference between Fuse and MCB?

Fuse melts and must be replaced after a fault. MCB simply trips and can be reset. MCB acts faster than a standard fuse.

Why is CE configuration widely used?

Common Emitter provides both high Voltage Gain and high Current Gain, yielding the highest overall Power Gain among BJT configs.
