⚡ UNIT I — DC & AC CIRCUITS

Master the fundamentals — backbone of every electrical system

Ⅰ
🔋 DC CIRCUITS
💡

Why This Topic Matters

DC circuits are everywhere! From your phone charger to solar panels to car batteries — understanding DC is your first step into electronics.

🎯 Real-world: Power supply design, battery systems, automotive electronics, embedded systems

🧠

Intuition First

Think of electricity like water in pipes:

  • Voltage (V) = Water pressure pushing through pipes
  • Current (I) = Volume of water flowing per second
  • Resistance (R) = Narrowness of the pipe restricting flow
🔌

Core Circuit Elements

⚡ Resistor (R)

Opposes current flow. Converts electrical energy to heat. Unit: Ohm (Ω)

🧲 Inductor (L)

Stores energy in magnetic field. Opposes change in current. Unit: Henry (H)

🔋 Capacitor (C)

Stores energy in electric field. Opposes change in voltage. Unit: Farad (F)

⚡

Formula Zone

Ohm's Law VERY HIGH PYQ
V = I × R
V→ Voltage (Volts, V)
I→ Current (Amperes, A)
R→ Resistance (Ohms, Ω)
🚀 VIR Triangle: Cover what you want to find — it shows the formula automatically!
Power Formulas HIGH PYQ
P = V × I = I² × R = V² / R
P→ Power (Watts, W)
🔗

Kirchhoff's Laws

KCL — Kirchhoff's Current Law VERY HIGH PYQ
Σ I(in) = Σ I(out)

💡 What comes in must go out! Like a traffic junction — cars entering = cars leaving.

KVL — Kirchhoff's Voltage Law VERY HIGH PYQ
Σ V = 0 (around any closed loop)

💡 Energy is conserved in a loop! Like hiking — if you return to start, net elevation change = 0.

⚙️

Circuit Configurations

Series Circuit
R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
CurrentSame everywhere
VoltageDivides across resistors
💡 Christmas lights — if one breaks, all go out!
Parallel Circuit
1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
VoltageSame across all
CurrentDivides through branches
💡 Home wiring — one device off doesn't affect others!
🎯

Superposition Theorem

Multiple sources? Find effect of each one separately, then add!

  1. Consider one source at a time — replace other voltage sources with short circuits, current sources with open circuits
  2. Calculate current/voltage due to that single source
  3. Repeat for every source
  4. Add all contributions algebraically (watch directions!)

⚠️ Common Mistakes:

  • Forgetting to replace sources properly
  • Ignoring current direction when adding
  • Applying superposition to power (doesn't work for nonlinear!)
⭐

PYQ Analysis

  • Series/parallel resistance calculations 10+ times
  • KVL/KCL circuit solving 15+ times
  • Superposition theorem numericals 8+ times
  • Power calculations 12+ times
⚡ DC Circuits — Quick Revision
  • ✅ V = IR · P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • ✅ KCL: Current in = Current out at junction
  • ✅ KVL: Sum of voltages around loop = 0
  • ✅ Series: R_total = R₁+R₂, same current, voltage divides
  • ✅ Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂, same voltage, current divides
  • ✅ Superposition: one source at a time, then algebraic sum
🌊 AC CIRCUITS
💡

Why This Topic Matters

AC powers your entire world! Home electricity, power grid, motors, generators — all AC. This is real electrical engineering.

🧠

Intuition First

DC vs AC — the core difference:

  • DC — River flowing one direction constantly
  • AC — Ocean waves — up, down, repeat forever!

💡 Why AC? Easier to generate, transmit long distances, and transform to different voltages!

📊

AC Waveform Parameters

Standard AC Equation
v(t) = Vm · sin(ωt + φ)
Vm→ Peak/Amplitude (maximum voltage)
ω→ Angular frequency (rad/s)
φ→ Phase angle (degrees or rad)
Frequency & Time Period HIGH PYQ
f = 1/T · ω = 2πf
f→ Frequency (Hz)
T→ Time period (seconds)
🇮🇳 India: 50 Hz · USA: 60 Hz
Critical Values VERY HIGH PYQ
Vrms = 0.707 × Vm
Vavg = 0.637 × Vm
Form F= 1.11 (Vrms/Vavg)
Peak F= 1.414 (Vm/Vrms)

💡 What is RMS? The equivalent DC value that produces the same heating effect!

Your home 230V is RMS — actual peak voltage = 230 × √2 ≈ 325V!

Memory trick for values: 0.707 · 0.637 · 1.11 · 1.414

📐

R, L, C Phasor Diagrams

Pure R Circuit

V and I are IN PHASE (φ = 0°)

Both reach peak at same time. Power is always positive.

Pure L Circuit

V LEADS I by 90°

ELI — E leads I in L. Reactance: XL = 2πfL

Pure C Circuit

I LEADS V by 90°

ICE — I leads E in C. Reactance: XC = 1/(2πfC)

🎯 Memory Trick: "ELI the ICE man"

ELI → E (Voltage) Leads I (Current) in L (Inductor)

ICE → I (Current) Leads E (Voltage) in C (Capacitor)

⚙️

Impedance

Impedance of R-L-C Circuit VERY HIGH PYQ
Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²)
Z→ Impedance (Ohms) — total AC opposition
XL→ Inductive reactance = 2πfL
XC→ Capacitive reactance = 1/(2πfC)
Phase Angle HIGH PYQ
φ = tan⁻¹((XL − XC) / R)

✅ XL > XC → Inductive (V leads)  |  ✅ XC > XL → Capacitive (I leads)

✅ XL = XC → Resonance! (Z = R, purely resistive)

⚡

Power in AC Circuits

Active Power VERY HIGH
P = VI·cos(φ)

Unit: Watts (W) — what you pay for!

Reactive Power
Q = VI·sin(φ)

Unit: VAR — bounces back and forth

Apparent Power
S = V × I

Unit: VA — S² = P² + Q²

Power Factor VERY HIGH PYQ
pf = cos(φ) = P/S = R/Z

Range: 0 to 1 · Ideal: 1.0 · Industry target: >0.9

⚠️ Low power factor = higher current = thicker cables = higher cost!

⚠️ Common Mistakes:

  • Using DC formulas in AC without reactance
  • Confusing RMS and peak values (0.707 × Vm!)
  • Adding AC voltages algebraically (must use phasors!)
  • Wrong units: P→W, Q→VAR, S→VA
⚡ AC Circuits — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Vrms = 0.707Vm · Vavg = 0.637Vm · FF = 1.11 · PF = 1.414
  • ✅ f = 1/T · ω = 2πf
  • ✅ XL = 2πfL · XC = 1/(2πfC)
  • ✅ Z = √(R² + (XL−XC)²)
  • ✅ ELI (V leads in L) · ICE (I leads in C)
  • ✅ P = VIcosφ (W) · Q = VIsinφ (VAR) · S = VI (VA)
  • ✅ S² = P² + Q² · pf = cosφ = P/S

⚙️ UNIT II — MACHINES & MEASURING INSTRUMENTS

How electrical machines convert energy and how we measure electrical quantities

Ⅱ
⚙️ DC MOTOR & DC GENERATOR
💡

Why This Topic Matters

Motors and generators are the heart of modern civilization! Electric vehicles, industrial machines, power plants — all use these principles.

🎯 Real-world: EVs, industrial automation, elevators, fans, pumps, power generation

🧠

The Beautiful Duality

  • DC Motor: Give electricity → Get rotation   (Electrical → Mechanical)
  • DC Generator: Give rotation → Get electricity   (Mechanical → Electrical)

💡 Mind-blowing: The SAME machine can work as both motor and generator!

🔌

Working Principle

DC Motor

Current-carrying conductor in magnetic field experiences force

📌 Fleming's Left Hand Rule

Thumb = Motion · Index = Field · Middle = Current

DC Generator

Moving conductor in magnetic field has EMF induced

📌 Fleming's Right Hand Rule

Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction

⚡

Formula Zone

EMF Equation (Motor Back EMF / Generator EMF) VERY HIGH PYQ
E = (P × Φ × N × Z) / (60 × A)
P→ Number of poles
Φ→ Flux per pole (Weber)
N→ Speed (RPM)
Z→ Total armature conductors
A→ Parallel paths (A=P for lap, A=2 for wave)
Motor Torque HIGH PYQ
T = (P × Φ × Ia × Z) / (2π × A)
T→ Torque (N·m)
Ia→ Armature current (A)
🔑 Key: Torque ∝ Φ × Ia — more flux or more current = more torque!

Types of DC Motors (by field connection):

Shunt Motor

Field parallel to armature. Constant speed. Used in lathes, fans.

Series Motor

Field in series. High starting torque. Used in cranes, traction.

Compound Motor

Both shunt + series field. Best of both. Used in elevators, compressors.

🎯 Applications:

  • DC Motors → EVs, industrial speed control, cranes, printers
  • DC Generators → Backup power, battery charging, welding machines
⚡ DC Machines — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Motor: Electrical→Mechanical (Left Hand Rule)
  • ✅ Generator: Mechanical→Electrical (Right Hand Rule)
  • ✅ E = PΦNZ/60A (same formula for both!)
  • ✅ Torque = PΦIaZ/2πA
  • ✅ Series motor: high torque · Shunt motor: constant speed
🔄 TRANSFORMER
💡

Why This Topic Matters

Transformers are the backbone of power transmission! Without them, electricity couldn't travel long distances. They're why you get safe 230V from 11kV power lines.

🎯 Real-world: Power grids, mobile chargers, welding machines, distribution systems, isolation

🧠

Intuition First

🎯 A Voltage Converter with NO Moving Parts!

Working: Changing current in primary coil → Changing magnetic field → Induces voltage in secondary coil (Mutual Induction)

Analogy: Like a gear system — trade voltage for current, like trading speed for force!

⚠️ Only works for AC — DC gives no changing field, no induction!

⚡

Formula Zone

Transformation Ratio VERY HIGH PYQ
K = N₂/N₁ = V₂/V₁ = I₁/I₂
K→ Transformation ratio
N₁,N₂→ Turns in primary, secondary
V₁,V₂→ Primary, secondary voltages
I₁,I₂→ Primary, secondary currents

K > 1 → Step-Up (V increases)  |  K < 1 → Step-Down (V decreases)  |  K = 1 → Isolation

EMF Equation HIGH PYQ
E = 4.44 × f × Φm × N
E→ Induced EMF (volts)
f→ Frequency (Hz)
Φm→ Maximum flux (Weber)
N→ Number of turns
🚀 Memory trick: "4.44" — remember three 4s!
Efficiency HIGH PYQ
η = (Output / Input) × 100%

Losses: Copper loss (I²R, variable) + Core loss (hysteresis + eddy current, fixed)

Practical: 95–99% efficiency for large transformers!

⚡ Transformer — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Works only on AC — mutual induction principle
  • ✅ K = N₂/N₁ = V₂/V₁ = I₁/I₂
  • ✅ EMF = 4.44 f Φm N
  • ✅ Step-up: K>1 · Step-down: K<1
  • ✅ Losses: copper + core (hysteresis + eddy current)
  • ✅ Efficiency: 95–99%
🔄 3-PHASE INDUCTION MOTOR
💡

Why This Topic Matters

The workhorse of industry! 3-phase induction motors power 80% of all industrial machinery — pumps, compressors, conveyors, fans.

🧠

Intuition First

🔄 The Magic of Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF):

3-phase AC in stator windings creates a magnetic field that rotates — even though the stator is stationary! This rotating field drags the rotor along.

💡 Why "Induction"? Rotor current is INDUCED (not directly connected), just like a transformer secondary!

🎯 Analogy: A spinning magnet pulling a metal disc without touching it.

⚡

Formula Zone

Synchronous Speed VERY HIGH PYQ
Ns = 120f / P
Ns→ Sync. speed (RPM)
f→ Frequency (Hz)
P→ No. of poles
50Hz, 4-pole: Ns = 1500 RPM
50Hz, 2-pole: Ns = 3000 RPM
Slip VERY HIGH PYQ
s = (Ns − N) / Ns
N→ Actual rotor speed
s→ Slip (2–5% at full load)
If slip = 0 → No induction, no torque! Rotor must lag.

Squirrel Cage Rotor

  • Simple, rugged, low maintenance
  • No external resistance connection
  • 90% of industrial motors

Slip Ring (Wound) Rotor

  • External resistance can be added
  • Better speed control
  • Higher starting torque
⚡ Induction Motor — Quick Revision
  • ✅ RMF is created by 3-phase supply to stator
  • ✅ Ns = 120f/P (synchronous speed)
  • ✅ Slip s = (Ns−N)/Ns · Typical: 2–5%
  • ✅ Squirrel cage: simple, rugged · Slip ring: speed control
  • ✅ Self-starting, no brushes, low maintenance
⚡ ALTERNATOR (AC Generator)

Alternators generate the AC electricity supplied to your home! Every power plant uses an alternator — from thermal to nuclear to hydro.

🧠

Intuition First

Reverse of Motor: Mechanical rotation → Changing magnetic field → Induced AC EMF

In alternators, the field (rotor) rotates while armature (stator) is stationary — opposite to DC generator!

EMF Equation HIGH PYQ
E = 4.44 × Kw × f × Φ × N
Kw→ Winding factor (≈ 0.95)
N→ Turns per phase
Frequency Equation HIGH PYQ
f = PN / 120
P→ Number of poles
N→ Speed in RPM
For 50Hz, 2-pole: N = 3000 RPM  |  4-pole: N = 1500 RPM

Alternator vs DC Generator:

FeatureAlternatorDC Generator
OutputACDC
Rotating partField (rotor)Armature (rotor)
CommutatorNot requiredRequired
Slip ringsRequiredNot required
📊 MEASURING INSTRUMENTS

You can't control what you can't measure! Measurement is fundamental to every electrical system. From your electricity meter to lab equipment — measurement keeps everything working.

🔬

PMMC vs Moving Iron vs Wheatstone

PMMC

DC only · High accuracy · Linear scale

Principle: Current-carrying coil in permanent magnet field experiences torque proportional to current

Limitation: DC only!

Moving Iron

AC & DC · Medium accuracy · Non-linear scale

Principle: Iron piece attracted or repelled by magnetic field. Two types: Attraction & Repulsion

Advantage: Works for both!

Wheatstone Bridge

DC · Very high accuracy · Resistance measurement

Principle: Bridge balance condition — no current through galvanometer

Best for: Precise R measurement

Wheatstone Bridge Balance HIGH PYQ
R₁/R₂ = R₃/R₄

At balance: No current through galvanometer → Unknown R = (R₂ × R₃) / R₁

⚡ Instruments — Quick Revision
  • ✅ PMMC: DC only, high accuracy, linear scale
  • ✅ Moving Iron: AC & DC, medium accuracy, non-linear
  • ✅ Wheatstone: Resistance measurement, R₁/R₂ = R₃/R₄

🌍 ENERGY RESOURCES

Energy powers civilization! Understanding sources helps you see where electricity comes from and why renewable energy is the future of engineering.

🏭 Conventional Sources

  • Coal — most used in India (thermal)
  • Petroleum & Natural Gas
  • Nuclear (uranium/thorium fission)
  • Hydro (large dams)

❌ Limited · Polluting · CO₂ emissions

🌿 Non-Conventional (Renewable)

  • ☀️ Solar — photovoltaic cells
  • 💨 Wind — turbines
  • 🌊 Tidal — ocean tides
  • 🔥 Geothermal — Earth's heat
  • 🌱 Biomass — organic matter

✅ Renewable · Clean · No CO₂ · Future!

⚡ POWER PLANTS
💧 Hydel (Hydroelectric) Power Plant ▼

Simple Idea: Falling water spins turbines → Generates electricity!

  1. Dam stores water at height (potential energy)
  2. Water released through penstock (high pressure pipes)
  3. High-speed water hits turbine blades → spins turbine
  4. Turbine drives generator → AC electricity!
  5. Water discharged to downstream river

✅ Clean · Low operating cost · 50+ year life · No fuel

❌ High initial cost · Depends on rainfall · Ecological impact

Examples: Bhakra Nangal, Tehri Dam, Sardar Sarovar

⚛️ Nuclear Power Plant ▼

Simple Idea: Split uranium atoms → Huge heat → Boil water → Steam spins turbine → Electricity!

✅ Tiny fuel needed · Huge power · No CO₂ emissions

❌ Radioactive waste · Safety risk · High initial cost

Examples: Tarapur, Kudankulam, Kaiga, Narora

☀️ Solar Power Plant ▼

Photovoltaic Effect: Sunlight photons hit semiconductor → Knock electrons free → Direct current flows!

PV (Photovoltaic)

Direct conversion. Solar panels on rooftops, solar farms. Most common.

Solar Thermal

Mirrors concentrate sunlight → Heat fluid → Steam turbine → Electricity

✅ Unlimited sun · Zero pollution · Low maintenance · Scalable

❌ Daylight only · Weather dependent · Storage needed

🇮🇳 India's Goal: 100 GW solar by 2030!

💨 Wind Power Plant ▼

Simple Idea: Wind spins aerodynamic blades → Turbine drives generator → AC electricity!

  1. Wind hits curved turbine blades (like airplane wings)
  2. Blades rotate the rotor shaft
  3. Gearbox increases speed to generator requirements
  4. Generator produces AC, fed to grid via transformer

✅ Clean · Low operating cost · Land usable for farming too

❌ Variable wind · Noise · High initial cost · Bird impact

Best locations: Coastal, hilltops — Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Rajasthan

💰 ELECTRICITY BILL CALCULATION

Money matters! Understanding electricity bills is life-long practical knowledge — and a very common exam question!

1 Unit = 1 kWh = 1000 Wh

Using 1000W (1kW) for 1 hour = 1 unit

100W bulb × 10 hours = 1 unit · 1000W heater × 1 hour = 1 unit · AC (2kW) × 30 min = 1 unit

Energy Consumption VERY HIGH PYQ
Energy (kWh) = Power (kW) × Time (hours)

Example: Fan 75W × 8h/day × 30 days = 0.075 × 8 × 30 = 18 kWh = 18 units

  • ✅ Highest consumers: AC, geyser, washing machine
⚠️ ELECTRICAL SAFETY

⚡ Even 50V AC can be lethal! Your home has 230V! Safety is not optional.

⚠️

Electric Shock

Current (mA)Effect on Human Body
1 mAJust perceptible (tingling)
5 mAMaximum "safe" threshold
10–20 mAPainful, loss of muscle control
50 mASevere pain, breathing difficulty
100–200 mAHeart stops — FATAL (ventricular fibrillation)

🚨 First Aid for Electric Shock:

  1. DO NOT touch the victim directly!
  2. Switch off power immediately at main switch
  3. If can't switch off — push victim with DRY wooden stick
  4. Check breathing and pulse
  5. Start CPR if needed
  6. Call ambulance (108) immediately
🔌

Protection Devices

🔌 Fuse — The Sacrificial Protector

Thin wire with low melting point. Excess current → Heats → Melts → Circuit breaks!

Material: Tin-lead alloy (low melting point)

Limitation: Must be replaced after operation (one-time use)

Types: Rewirable · Cartridge · HRC

⚡ MCB — Miniature Circuit Breaker

Automatically breaks circuit — no replacement needed, just reset!

Thermal: Bimetallic strip for overload protection

Magnetic: Electromagnet for short circuit (instant trip)

Ratings: 6A, 10A, 16A, 32A

🌍

Earthing (Grounding)

💡 What it does: Provides a safe path for leakage current to flow into the earth instead of through YOUR body!

Without earthing: Faulty appliance body = 230V. You touch it → Current through you → SHOCK!

With earthing: Leakage current flows safely through earth wire into ground. You are safe! ✅

Components:

  • Earth electrode: GI plate/copper rod buried in ground
  • Earth wire: Green/yellow wire, connects metal body to electrode
  • Earth pit: Moisture-rich pit, maintains low resistance

Target earth resistance: < 1 Ohm

🛡️ Safety Precautions:

  • ✅ Never work on live circuits
  • ✅ Switch off main before repairs
  • ✅ Use insulated tools
  • ✅ No wet hands near electricity
  • ✅ Check earthing of appliances
  • ✅ Correct MCB/fuse ratings
  • ❌ Never use copper wire as fuse
  • ❌ Never overload sockets
  • ❌ No electrical in bathroom
  • ❌ Ignore burning smell = danger!
  • ❌ No DIY electrical without knowledge
  • ❌ Never touch shock victim with bare hands
⚡ Safety — Quick Revision
  • ✅ 100mA through heart = FATAL
  • ✅ Fuse: melts, one-time, low melting point alloy
  • ✅ MCB: trips & resets, thermal + magnetic protection
  • ✅ Earthing: diverts leakage current to ground (earth resistance <1Ω)
  • ✅ First aid: DON'T touch victim, switch off power first

🔬 UNIT IV — SEMICONDUCTOR DEVICES

Enter the world of modern electronics — diodes, transistors, and amplifiers

Ⅳ
🔌 PN JUNCTION DIODE
💡

Why This Topic Matters

The foundation of all modern electronics! Diode is the simplest semiconductor device — understanding it unlocks transistors, ICs, and everything digital.

🎯 Real-world: Rectifiers (AC→DC), LED lighting, voltage clippers, solar panels, signal demodulation

🧠

Intuition First

🎯 The One-Way Valve of Electronics:

Diode allows current to flow in ONE direction only, like a check valve in water pipes.

P-side (Anode) — has holes (positive charge carriers)

N-side (Cathode) — has electrons (negative charge carriers)

At junction: Electrons and holes recombine → Depletion region forms → Acts as a barrier

⚙️

Biasing Conditions

Forward Bias (ON)

P connected to +ve, N to −ve

Barrier reduces → Current flows ✅

Cut-in voltage:

Silicon: ~0.7V · Germanium: ~0.3V

Reverse Bias (OFF)

P connected to −ve, N to +ve

Barrier increases → Only tiny leakage current ✅

Breakdown voltage: If exceeded → Reverse breakdown!

⚡

Diode Equation

Ideal Diode Equation HIGH PYQ
I = Is(e^(V/VT) − 1)
Is→ Reverse saturation current (tiny)
V→ Applied voltage
VT→ Thermal voltage ≈ 26mV at room temp
🔌

Half Wave & Full Wave Rectifiers

Rectifier = AC → DC Converter using diodes!

Half Wave Rectifier
Vdc = Vm/π = 0.318Vm

Uses 1 diode · Passes only +ve half cycle · Efficiency: 40.6%

Full Wave Rectifier
Vdc = 2Vm/π = 0.636Vm

Uses 4 diodes (bridge) · Passes both halves · Efficiency: 81.2%

⚡ PN Diode — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Forward bias: P→+ve, N→−ve, current flows
  • ✅ Reverse bias: P→−ve, N→+ve, no current
  • ✅ Si cut-in: 0.7V · Ge cut-in: 0.3V
  • ✅ HWR: Vdc = 0.318Vm · FWR: Vdc = 0.636Vm
  • ✅ Applications: Rectifiers, clippers, clampers, LEDs
⚡ ZENER DIODE

The voltage stabilizer! Zener diode maintains constant output voltage despite varying input — fundamental to every power supply.

🎯 Real-world: Voltage regulators, reference circuits, surge protection, power supplies

🧠

Intuition First

🎯 The Voltage Clamp:

A normal diode breaks down in reverse bias. A Zener is designed to break down at a specific voltage (Zener voltage Vz) — and hold that voltage precisely!

Analogy: Like a pressure relief valve — once pressure hits the set point, it holds it constant no matter what!

Zener as Voltage Regulator VERY HIGH PYQ
Vout = Vz (constant)
Vz→ Zener breakdown voltage (fixed by design)
Rs→ Series resistor to limit current

Series resistor: Rs = (Vin − Vz) / Iz

Power dissipation in Zener: Pz = Vz × Iz

Normal Diode vs Zener

  • Normal diode: Breaks down & destroyed in reverse
  • Zener: Designed to work in reverse breakdown safely
  • Zener symbol: Bent cathode line (Z shape)

Applications

  • Voltage regulators (5V, 3.3V, etc.)
  • Voltage reference circuits
  • Overvoltage protection/clippers
⚡ Zener Diode — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Works in reverse breakdown — maintains constant Vz
  • ✅ Used as voltage regulator: Vout = Vz
  • ✅ Series resistor Rs = (Vin−Vz)/Iz
  • ✅ Symbol: bent cathode (like Z)
🔬 BJT TRANSISTOR
💡

Why This Topic Matters

The transistor changed the world! Modern smartphones have 10 billion+ transistors. Every computer, phone, and digital device relies on transistors.

🎯 Real-world: Amplifiers, switches, oscillators, logic gates, all digital ICs

🧠

Intuition First

🎯 The Current-Controlled Current Source:

A small base current controls a much larger collector current — like a valve controlling a water flow!

Two types: NPN (current flows N→P→N) · PNP (current flows P→N→P)

Three terminals: Base (B) · Collector (C) · Emitter (E)

🔑 Key Rule: Ib × β = Ic (small base current amplified β times!)

⚡

BJT Parameters

Current Relationship VERY HIGH PYQ
IE = IB + IC
IE→ Emitter current (largest)
IB→ Base current (smallest, control current)
IC→ Collector current
Current Gain Parameters VERY HIGH PYQ
β = IC / IB  (CE gain)
α = IC / IE  (CB gain)
β (hFE)→ CE current gain (typical: 20–500)
α→ CB current gain (α = β/(β+1), always <1)

📢 CE AMPLIFIER

Amplifiers make weak signals strong! Microphones, radio receivers, audio systems — all need amplification. CE amplifier is the fundamental building block.

🧠

Intuition First

🎯 Small input → Big output, same shape!

A tiny AC signal at the base gets amplified at the collector — like using a small lever to move a big rock.

Key feature: CE amplifier gives 180° phase shift — output is inverted!

⚡

Circuit & Formulas

Voltage Gain VERY HIGH PYQ
Av = −β × RC / re
Av→ Voltage gain (negative = 180° phase shift)
RC→ Collector resistance
re→ AC emitter resistance ≈ 26mV/IE
Load Line & Q-Point HIGH PYQ
VCE = VCC − IC × RC

Q-point (Quiescent point): The DC operating point — where transistor sits without any AC signal

For undistorted amplification: Q-point must be at middle of load line

Characteristics of CE Amplifier:

  • High voltage gain AND current gain → maximum power gain
  • Phase reversal (180° shift) between input and output
  • Medium input impedance, medium output impedance
  • Most widely used configuration for audio amplifiers
⚡ CE Amplifier — Quick Revision
  • ✅ Av = −β × RC/re (negative = 180° phase shift)
  • ✅ VCE = VCC − IC×RC (load line equation)
  • ✅ Q-point: middle of load line for max undistorted output
  • ✅ CE: high current gain, high voltage gain, 180° phase reversal

Repeated Division by 2

📋 DIGITAL CODES

Codes solve real problems! Different codes handle error detection, weighted representation, and efficient digital communication.

BCD Code (Binary Coded Decimal) ▼

Each decimal digit represented by its 4-bit binary equivalent separately.

Example: 9₁₀ = 1001 in BCD · 25₁₀ = 0010 0101 in BCD

Valid BCD digits: 0000 to 1001 (0–9) · 1010 to 1111 are INVALID in BCD!

Use: Digital clocks, calculators, 7-segment displays

Excess-3 Code (XS-3) ▼

BCD + 3 added to each digit. Self-complementing code!

Conversion: Take BCD of digit, add 0011 (3)

Example: 5 → BCD = 0101 → XS-3 = 0101+0011 = 1000

Key property: 9's complement obtained by inverting all bits!

Gray Code ▼

Only ONE bit changes between consecutive numbers — reduces errors in mechanical systems!

Binary to Gray Conversion HIGH PYQ
MSB stays same. Each other bit = XOR of that binary bit and previous binary bit

Example: Binary 1011 → Gray

G3=B3=1 · G2=B3⊕B2=1⊕0=1 · G1=B2⊕B1=0⊕1=1 · G0=B1⊕B0=1⊕1=0

Binary 1011 = Gray 1110

Applications: Rotary encoders, error reduction in digital sensors, optical encoders

Hamming Code (Error Correction) ▼

Adds parity bits at specific positions to DETECT and CORRECT single-bit errors!

Parity bit positions: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16 ... (powers of 2)

For n data bits: Need r parity bits where 2^r ≥ n + r + 1

For 4 data bits: r = 3 parity bits → total 7-bit Hamming code

Error detection: Check all parity positions → Syndrome gives error position!

Applications: RAM memory error correction, network transmission, satellite communication

⚡ Digital Codes — Quick Revision
  • ✅ BCD: each decimal digit = 4-bit binary (0000–1001 valid)
  • ✅ XS-3: BCD + 3, self-complementing, 9's comp = bit inversion
  • ✅ Gray: only 1 bit changes at a time, B→G: MSB same, others XOR
  • ✅ Hamming: parity bits at 2^n positions, detects AND corrects 1-bit errors

📐 BOOLEAN ALGEBRA

Boolean algebra simplifies logic circuits! Instead of using more gates, simplify expressions to use fewer — saving cost and power.

⚡

Key Theorems

De Morgan's Theorems VERY HIGH PYQ
(A·B)̄ = Ā + B̄
(A+B)̄ = Ā · B̄

Rule: Break the bar, change the sign!

AND ↔ OR when bar is broken across

Basic Identities HIGH PYQ
A+0=A · A·1=A · A+1=1 · A·0=0
A+A=A · A·A=A · A+Ā=1 · A·Ā=0

Idempotent: A+A=A · A·A=A

Complement: A+Ā=1 · A·Ā=0

Important Laws HIGH PYQ
Commutative: A+B = B+A · A·B = B·A
Associative: (A+B)+C = A+(B+C)
Distributive: A·(B+C) = A·B + A·C
Absorption: A+A·B = A · A·(A+B) = A
⚡ Boolean Algebra — Quick Revision
  • ✅ De Morgan's 1: (AB)̄ = Ā+B̄ · De Morgan's 2: (A+B)̄ = Ā·B̄
  • ✅ A+Ā = 1 · A·Ā = 0 · A+A = A · A·A = A
  • ✅ Absorption: A+AB = A · A(A+B) = A
  • ✅ Double complement: (Ā)̄ = A

Flip-flops are the memory cells of digital circuits! Unlike combinational circuits (output depends only on current input), flip-flops REMEMBER the previous state.

📟 REGISTERS & COUNTERS
💡

Registers

Registers are temporary data storage! Your CPU's registers hold data being processed. Shift registers are used in serial communication.

SISO — Serial In, Serial Out

Data enters and exits one bit at a time. Used in serial communication, time delay circuits.

SIPO — Serial In, Parallel Out

Serial input, all bits output simultaneously. Serial to parallel converter!

PISO — Parallel In, Serial Out

All bits loaded at once, output one by one. Parallel to serial converter!

PIPO — Parallel In, Parallel Out

Fastest! Load and output all bits simultaneously. CPU data registers.

🔢

Counters

🎯 Counters count clock pulses!

Built from flip-flops chained together. Each clock pulse increments (or decrements) the count.

n flip-flops → can count 2^n states (0 to 2^n−1)

Ripple (Asynchronous) Counter HIGH PYQ
Modulus = 2^n

Each FF triggered by output of previous FF.

Simple but slower (ripple delay).

Example: 4-bit counter counts 0 to 15 (mod-16)

Synchronous Counter HIGH PYQ
All FFs triggered simultaneously

All FFs get clock at same time → Faster!

More complex but high-speed operation.

Used in: CPUs, high-speed digital systems
